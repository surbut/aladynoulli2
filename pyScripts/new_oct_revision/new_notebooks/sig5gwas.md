### Known Associations (from literature):
- **HDL cholesterol levels** (multiple GWAS)
- **Lipid metabolism traits**
  - **Gene-gene interactions** with HMGCR affecting HDL-C
- **Coronary heart disease** (in some populations)
- **Metabolic syndrome traits**
  
  ### Why This Is "Novel" in Your Analysis:
  **This is the KEY insight for reviewers!**
  
  Rs1532085 was:
  - ❌ NOT significant in any of your 6 individual CV trait GWAS (Angina, MI, Hypercholesterolemia, etc.)
- ✅ BUT significant in your cardiovascular signature (SIG5)

### What This Demonstrates:

1. **Your signature captures lipid-mediated CV risk** that isn't fully represented by individual diagnostic codes

2. **Joint modeling reveals pleiotropic effects:** The locus affects CV disease through lipid metabolism pathways that influence multiple CV conditions simultaneously - only detectable when you model them together

3. **Biological validity:** This isn't a statistical artifact - it's a real biological signal (lipid metabolism → cardiovascular disease) that your method successfully captured

### Perfect Response to Reviewer #2:

> "A compelling example is rs1532085 near the LIPC (hepatic lipase) gene on chromosome 15. This variant is well-established in the literature as associated with HDL cholesterol and lipid metabolism (refs), biological pathways central to cardiovascular disease pathogenesis. However, in our dataset, rs1532085 was NOT genome-wide significant in any of the six individual cardiovascular trait GWAS (angina, MI, hypercholesterolemia, coronary atherosclerosis, acute IHD, or chronic IHD). It only emerged as significant when we analyzed the cardiovascular signature, which models these related conditions jointly. This demonstrates that our signature-based approach can reveal biologically meaningful loci that operate through pleiotropic effects on multiple cardiovascular processes - effects that are individually too weak to detect but become statistically apparent through joint modeling."
